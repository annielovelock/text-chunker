FAQs

Q: What is the character limit for ChatGPT input?
A: ChatGPT has a maximum input limit of 4096 tokens, which includes both input and output tokens. The number of characters in a token varies, but on average, there are about 4 characters per token, including spaces and punctuation. This tool creates chunks of around 4000 characters, which ChatGPT should be able to comfortably process.

Q: What happens if I exceed the character limit?
A: If the text you provide exceeds the character limit, you'll need to truncate, omit, or shorten the text until it fits within the limit. Otherwise, ChatGPT will not be able to process the input.

Q: Can I split my long text into multiple parts and send them separately to ChatGPT?
A: Yes, that's what this tool is for! You can split your long text into smaller chunks that fit within the character limit and send them separately to ChatGPT. However, be aware that the context from the previous chunk may not always carry over, which could result in less coherent responses.

Q: How does the output character limit affect ChatGPT responses?
A: The remaining available tokens after considering the input tokens determine the output character limit. If your input is very long, the space available for the generated response will be shorter, which could lead to incomplete or less informative answers.

Q: How can I estimate the number of tokens in my text?
A: While an exact estimation requires tokenization using the ChatGPT tokenizer, you can get a rough estimate by counting the number of characters, including spaces and punctuation, and dividing by 4 (the average number of characters per token).

Q: Is there a recommended text length for best results with ChatGPT?
A: For optimal results, it's best to keep your input text concise and focused on the question or topic at hand. Shorter inputs leave more room for ChatGPT to generate a detailed and relevant response.
